Why Digitize Oromo Folklore?

April 10, 2026 | Ame Aliyi
Oromo folklore is a treasure of wisdom, history, and identity. Yet, much remains undocumented...

Oral traditions include folk tales, proverbs, songs, and poetry. Digitization ensures preservation and global access. It also enables NLP research and AI translation. This project at Oda Bultum University is just the beginning.

Key benefits: permanent storage, easy search, remote access, academic integration. Join me in making Oromo culture digital and immortal.

My Journey as a Computer Scientist in a Folklore Department

February 28, 2026 | Ame Aliyi
When I first joined the Department of Oromo Folklore, many asked: "What does a CS grad do there?" I build bridges...

I digitize manuscripts, create databases, record oral stories, and teach digital skills. The Digital Archive now holds 200+ folk tales and 100+ proverbs. I've also introduced digital humanities workshops.

Innovation and tradition are partners. I invite tech professionals to contribute to African cultural preservation.

The Power of Oromo Proverbs in Daily Life

March 15, 2026 | Ame Aliyi
Mammaaksa Oromo are not just sayings – they are ethical guides and collective wisdom passed for generations...

Proverbs like "Ilkaan hin qabne, lafee cabsa" teach resilience. "Dhugaa dubbadhu, utuu jiraattu du'a" emphasizes truth. We must preserve and study them systematically.

Our digital archive includes 20+ proverbs with English translations, and we continue to add more from community elders.
